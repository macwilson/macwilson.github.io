"AntiFreeze":

University of Waterloo, Freezing of Gait Project
> Team Member

AntiFreeze is a project designed to detect freezing of gait in Parkinson's patients and apply a stimulus that allows them to continue walking.

I focussed mostly on designing, building and maintaining the circuit that connects an accelerometer and a motor to a microcontroller, which allows the detection of "shuffling" before freezing begins as well as the application of the vibrating stimulus.

I also assisted with freezing of gait data collection and analysis in Matlab in order to generate framework concepts for the signal processing algorithm, which was eventually written in C for the microcontroller, as well as report writing, research, and presentation for the final solution.

Skills Used: Circuit design/breadboarding, Matlab, C, signal processing, object oriented programming, teamwork

Sept-Dec 2016, Waterloo ON.

Furniture Refurbishing and Design:

Personal Project

Furniture design/construction is a side hobby of mine that started with the drawers above and continues today - I often see if I can build a piece of furniture instead of buying it. These projects save me money, bring me pleasure, and allow me to get creative in an unusual way. I pride myself on the small details of a piece, such as routered designs in the door, selection of the right hardware, or tiny designs on drawer legs created with a lathe.

The set of drawers above was refurbished from a broken, discarded set from the 1960s. I aimed to preserve or improve original details while fixing broken components and revamping the overall style. I later constructed a set of shelves in a matching design from scratch.

Most recently I constructed a desk from a discarded wooden door with several matching shelving units built from scratch.

Skills Used: Design sketching, creativity, machining (notable tools: drill press, band saw, circular saw, lathe, sander, router)
