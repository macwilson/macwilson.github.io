Plan International:
Co-Founder and Developer for "NatalNet"

After winning the first place prize at the Break Inequality Hackathon 2016, NatalNet moved into the implementation phase as a not-for-profit startup.

Now working with Plan International (Canada) and Developers Without Borders, I continued to develop front and back end software for the prize-winning web-app and SMS interface connection, seamlessly integrating several APIs.

NatalNet aims to reduce the child and maternal mortality rates in rural Bangladesh by connecting mothers to trained community healthcare workers for the care and information they need.

Skills Used: Javascript (Node.js), HTML, CSS, teamwork, object oriented programming, app design

Nov 2016-Present, Toronto ON.

Vision and Image Processing Research Group:
Research Assistant

I redesigned the optical, mechanical and electrical systems of a novel hemodynamic imaging system.

Additionally I proposed and integrated thermal imaging functionality into the system for complete metabolic biomarker monitoring, as outlined in a conference presentation and publication.

Lastly I investigated the electrical and optical requirements for implementation of multispectrality in order for the system to gather deeper blood nutrient data.

Skills Used: SolidWorks, Matlab, Arduino, circuit design and analysis, PCB design, machining, 3D printing, signal processing, data analysis, experiment design, academic writing, communication/presentation skills

May-Dec 2016, Waterloo ON.
